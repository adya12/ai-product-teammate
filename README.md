## Overview

AI Product Teammate is designed to simulate how senior product managers structure ambiguous problems into actionable product strategies.

Instead of generating generic AI text, the system outputs:

- Clear problem framing
- Behavioral user segmentation
- Risk-aware product decision frameworks
- Measurable product metrics
- Tradeoff analysis
- Experiment design
- Actionable product strategy recommendations

The focus is on **AI-assisted product reasoning**, not just text generation.

---

## Motivation

Product teams often start with vague ideas:

- "Customers abandon checkout."
- "Chargebacks increased after BNPL launch."
- "Conversion drops during payment."

Turning ambiguity into structured thinking requires experience and systems thinking.

This project explores how AI can:

- accelerate product thinking
- structure ambiguity into decision frameworks
- simulate senior product strategy workflows.

### Example Input

Merchants are seeing increased chargebacks after introducing Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL), but disabling BNPL reduces conversion and average order value. Design an AI-driven product strategy that balances fraud risk, merchant revenue, and customer experience without increasing operational complexity.

### Example Output

## Problem Framing
- **Underlying problem and why now:** As BNPL adoption grows, merchants face increased chargeback rates, often triggered by legitimate transactions being disputed by customers. This has two main consequences: (1) revenue loss due to chargebacks, and (2) decreased trust from merchants who are hesitant to offer BNPL due to its perceived risk. The combination of these factors requires an immediate solution.
- **Where in the journey does it occur and what causes it?:** Chargebacks stemming from BNPL are primarily triggered by customer disputes after the initial payment period has expired and subsequent installment payments have begun. This creates opportunities for legitimate transactions to be disputed as customers seek refunds or dispute unauthorized payments.

## Behavioral User Segments
- **Segments and characteristics:**
  1. **Early Adopters** (high-velocity): High-value customers who quickly take advantage of BNPL but may lack detailed payment histories. **Motivation:** High conversion rates, **risk pattern:** High risk of legitimate disputes, **what they need:** Education on installment payments and easy payment tracking.
  2. **Price-Sensitive Repeat Buyers** (medium-velocity): Customers who return for high-AOV purchases but seek discounts. **Motivation:** High average order value, **risk pattern:** Higher risk of intentional disputes, **what they need:** Personalized, transparent payment options and incentives for repeat business.
  3. **First-Time Buyers** (low-velocity): Low-risk customers with little credit history. **Motivation:** Lower average order value, **risk pattern:** Lower risk of dispute, **what they need:** Clear communication of repayment terms and transparent BNPL options.
  4. **High-Value Repeat Buyers** (high-velocity): Customers who purchase high-ticket items frequently. **Motivation:** Very high conversion rates, **risk pattern:** Higher risk of disputes, **what they need:** Priority customer service, payment tracking, and exclusive promotions.
  5. **Cross-Border Buyers** (medium-velocity): Customers who make international transactions. **Motivation:** Unique product offerings, **risk pattern:** Higher risk of intentional disputes, **what they need:** Real-time payment tracking and streamlined cross-border payment options.

## Core Metrics
- **Business metrics:**
  - Conversion rate
  - Average order value (AOV)
  - Revenue lift
- **Risk metrics:**
  - Chargeback rate
  - Fraud loss rate
  - False positives/decline rate
- **Experience metrics:**
  - Latency
  - Step drop-off
  - Customer service contacts

## Key Tradeoffs
- **Approval vs. losses:** Balancing the need to prevent chargebacks while minimizing revenue loss due to overly conservative payment authorization.
- **Friction vs. trust:** Reducing the need for manual customer service interventions while maintaining a seamless and transparent payment experience.
- **Speed vs. accuracy:** Ensuring timely payment authorizations without sacrificing accuracy in the face of high-pressure sales cycles.

## AI Mechanism
- **Decisioning loop:**
  1. Signals: Merchant, customer, and transactional data.
  2. Risk/intent score: AI-driven risk assessment for chargeback likelihood.
  3. Action: Dynamic payment options (e.g., instant BNPL, 3D Secure, delayed payment plans).
  4. Feedback loop: Continuous monitoring and improvement of AI-powered decisioning through machine learning.

- **Dynamically changing the user flow:**
  - **Step-up verification:** Automated requests for additional customer information or ID verification to mitigate risk.
  - **Payment option ordering:** Personalized payment options presented to customers based on their risk profile and transaction history.
  - **Retry guidance:** In-app messages to guide customers through payment retries, reducing friction and customer support interactions.

## Solution Strategy
- **End-to-end approach:** Detection ( AI-powered risk assessment), decisioning (dynamic payment options), UX interventions ( step-up verification, payment option ordering), merchant controls (real-time monitoring and alerts), and transparency (in-app messages).

- **Operational scalability:** AI-driven solutions integrate seamlessly with existing infrastructure, minimizing the need for manual updates and interventions.

## Recommended Experiments
- **Experiment 1:** Compare chargeback rates between customers who have been offered dynamic payment options vs. those who have not. **Success criteria:** 20% reduction in chargeback rate.
- **Experiment 2:** Evaluate the impact of a step-up verification strategy on false positives/decline rate. **Success criteria:** 30% reduction in false positives/decline rate.
- **Experiment 3:** Analyze the conversion rate and revenue lift of customers who opt-in for a streamlined payment process (e.g., instant BNPL) vs. those who do not. **Success criteria:** 25% increase in revenue lift.
- **Experiment 4:** Compare customer satisfaction ratings between customers who experience a seamless payment process (0-step drop-off, low latency) and those who do not. **Success criteria:** 15% improvement in customer satisfaction rating.

## Product Recommendations
- **Immediate priorities:**
  1. Ship dynamic payment options (experiment 1 and 2 targets).
  2. Implement automated step-up verification (experiment 2 target).
- **Near-term enhancements:**
  1. Introduce a retry guidance system to reduce friction (reduce step drop-off).
  2. Develop in-app messages to guide customers through payment retries (reduce customer service contacts).
- **Future exploration:**
  1. Implement AI-driven merchant controls to identify and flag high-risk transactions before they happen.
  2. Develop a real-time payment tracking system to provide customers with transparency on their payment history and repayment terms.
